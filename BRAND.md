# Linelo Brand Guide

## Brand in One Sentence

Linelo is the calm inbox that helps people finish email by showing one unread message at a time and using AI to quietly guide triage and replies.

## Core Idea

Communication should serve your life, not consume it.

Linelo does not help users stay on top of email. It helps them finish it.

## Positioning

Linelo is an open-source inbox for people who want communication to feel finite. It combines a one-message-at-a-time workflow, AI-assisted triage and reply support, and pragmatic control over infrastructure to make email feel clear, calm, and finishable.

## Mission

Help users reach inbox zero with less stress, less ambiguity, and less time spent inside communication tools.

## Product Promise

Finish your inbox calmly.

## Audience

- People overwhelmed by unread messages
- Professionals with high email volume
- Founders, operators, consultants, and managers
- Technical and privacy-conscious users who value open source and self-hosting

## The Problem With Email

### Email overload and volume

Many people receive dozens to hundreds of emails each day: newsletters, automated notifications, internal threads, promotional messages, CC'd digests, alerts, and more. The sheer volume can feel overwhelming and make it harder to spot what actually matters.

### Lack of hierarchy

Most inboxes do a poor job of distinguishing urgency. Important messages get mixed with irrelevant ones, so people delay reading until they think they can handle the inbox properly. That moment often never comes.

### Stress and avoidance

A large unread count can create a small but persistent stress response. For some people, the immediate discomfort leads to avoidance, which increases backlog and makes the inbox feel even heavier the next time they return.

### Vigilance is the wrong incentive

Current email rewards vigilance. The more often people check, the more burden they feel. Linelo should reward completed actions instead, helping users move messages toward resolution rather than training them to stay alert.

## Brand Pillars

### 1. Resolution over management

Email is a task, not a destination. The goal is not to keep managing the inbox. The goal is to reach a finished state and move on.

### 2. Calm by design

Linelo should reduce pressure immediately. The product, interface, and language should feel quiet, focused, and controlled.

### 3. Attention protection

Linelo is a counter-addictive product. It should reduce compulsion, not create it.

This means:

- No dopamine loops
- No infinite scroll
- No urgency-driven notification design
- Batching over constant interruption
- Fewer decisions, clearer decisions

### 4. AI as quiet support

AI is not the headline experience. It works in the background to prioritize, summarize, and draft so users can decide faster and finish with less ambiguity.

### 5. Pragmatic control builds trust

Open source and self-hosting are practical advantages. They give users ownership of infrastructure and data, flexibility in deployment, and less dependence on a closed vendor.

### 6. Offbeat, not overproduced

Linelo should not feel slick, polished to death, or overly self-important. The brand can use dry humor, light irreverence, and slightly offbeat phrasing, but always in service of a clear point.

## Workflow Principle

Every message should move toward a clear end state:

- Reply
- Delegate
- Defer
- Remove

Not all messages deserve attention. AI and rules should help create a single priority lane instead of a noisy backlog.

## Differentiators

- One unread email at a time
- Clear emphasis on finishing, not managing
- AI plus rules for prioritization and reply support
- Minimal reliance on folders, labels, and manual systems
- Open-source, self-hosted deployment
- Designed to protect attention, not compete for it

## Messaging Principles

- Lead with resolution, not feature volume
- Prefer concrete outcomes over abstract productivity claims
- Emphasize less overwhelm, less ambiguity, and less time spent on email
- Describe AI as support, not spectacle
- Frame open source and self-hosting as pragmatic control
- Keep language simple, direct, and low-drama
- Use understated humor or offbeat phrasing only when the message stays clear
- Do not imply users should be more available, more responsive, or more engaged with email

## Brand Personality

Linelo should sound:

- Calm
- Clear
- Competent
- Focused
- Respectful
- Practical
- Deliberate
- Dryly funny
- Offbeat
- Unpretentious

Linelo should not sound:

- Hype-driven
- Hyperactive
- Alarmist
- Gamified
- Clever for its own sake
- Overly corporate
- Artificially friendly
- Slick
- Overproduced
- Self-important

## Visual Direction

### Primary Colors

- Dark slate (`#1C1F24`)
- Off-white (`#F5F5F3`)

### Accent

- Muted green (`#6E8F7A`) for a playful touch

### Typography

- Sans-serif
- Clean
- Modern
- Neutral

### Layout Principles

- One primary action per screen
- High whitespace
- Clear hierarchy
- Minimal visual noise

## Tone of Voice

### Use

- Short, declarative sentences
- Calm, grounded wording
- Specific outcomes
- Language of focus, resolution, and control
- Practical explanations of how the product behaves
- Occasional dry humor or lightly offbeat phrasing

### Avoid

- "Supercharge your productivity"
- "Revolutionary AI"
- "Never worry again"
- "Chat with your inbox"
- "All-in-one communication magic"
- Jokes or irony that make the message harder to understand
- Language that exaggerates, gamifies, or overpromises

## Terminology

Use consistently:

- "finish your inbox"
- "one unread email at a time"
- "AI-assisted triage and replies"
- "open source"
- "calm"
- "resolution"
- "attention protection"

Avoid:

- "AI co-pilot" when used vaguely
- "chat with your inbox"
- "productivity engine"
- "organize everything"

## Core Copy

### Short Description

A calm, self-hosted inbox built to help you finish email, not manage it forever.

### Elevator Pitch

Linelo is an open-source, self-hosted email client built around a simple idea: finish your inbox. Instead of confronting users with a wall of unread messages, it shows one unread email at a time and uses AI plus rules to guide prioritization and replies, so communication feels finite, clear, and resolvable.

### Tagline Directions

- Finish your inbox.
- Email. Done.
- Less inbox. More life.
- One email at a time.

## Future Expansion

Linelo starts with email, but the same resolution-first workflow can extend to other communication channels later. Expansion should only happen if it preserves the core promise: less overwhelm, clearer prioritization, fewer interruptions, and faster resolution.

## Strategic Guardrails

- Do not position Linelo as a chat-first or social product
- Do not make AI the main story at the expense of calm and resolution
- Do not frame responsiveness as the goal
- Do not overemphasize folders, labels, or manual organization
- Do not turn open source and self-hosting into abstract ideology
- Do not expand to other channels unless the resolution-first story still holds

## Brand Summary

Linelo stands for calm, control, and resolution. It helps users finish communication without drowning in it by protecting attention, reducing ambiguity, and guiding each message toward a clear end state.
